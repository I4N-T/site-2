Productivity Report - September 2017

Each Month, I make a post detailing what I’ve been working on, what I’ve accomplished, and just what I’ve been up to. This month's banner image features a rainbow outside my office window.

Broad Overview

September 2017 is the thirteenth month in which I have tracked my productivity. My productivity level this month was ok, considering I missed several days due to vacation and a business trip. My main focus for the month was continuing to work on my office management game, tentatively titled Widget CEO.

  • Fitness: 26 hours
  • Software Development: 47 hours
  • Music: 21.5 hours
  • Other: 6.75 hours
  • Total productive hours: 101.25 hours

My average amount of productive hours per day (from all categories) was 3.27 hours.

My productivity level for the month of September 2017 was about average. I guess I can't complain, but it feels like I could have done more.

Software Development

My software development goals for the month of September was to:

  • Work hard on my new game, Widget CEO.

While I did put a decent amount of work into Widget CEO this month (47 hours), I unfortunately (again) have it in a bit of a broken state at the moment as I work on a new pathfinding algorithm. What that means is that there will be no video this month.

But now with the end of the year quickly approaching, I really need to decide what it is I want to do with Widget CEO. While it has been a great project, a tremendous learning experience, and has a ton of potential to actually be a fun game, I think I will aim to wrap it up by the end of October. Now, I very well may come back to Widget CEO in the future, but I want to at least have something resembling a complete project by the end of the month. More or less, what I have left to implement are pathfinding, title screen, some balancing, and a few other minor details.

My goal for October 2017 will be to bring Widget CEO to a (temporary?) close.

I switched up my lifting routine again. Now I am doing a short full body routine 4 days a week. I'll give an update in next month's report with how it's working out for me.
